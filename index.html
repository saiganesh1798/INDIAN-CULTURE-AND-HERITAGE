About India

Why is India Oldest Civilization ?

According to reserch, "The Indus Valley Civilization, one of the earliest urban civilizations in the world, flourished in the northwestern part of the Indian subcontinent around 3300 BCE. This ancient civilization showcased remarkable advancements in town planning, sanitation, and trade. Over the centuries, India has witnessed the influx of various people, from the Indo-Aryan tribes to the Mughals and the British. Each wave of migration and conquest has contributed to the country's cultural diversity and historical depth."

India's cultural diversity is a testament to its history of migration and conquest. From the ancient Indus Valley Civilization to the Mughal Empire, various cultures have contributed to the country's rich heritage. This diversity is reflected in India's vibrant arts, music, literature, and cuisine. It has achieved all-round socio-economic progress during the last 65 years of its Independence.

Tajmahal is symbol of love

An immense mausoleum of white marble, built in Agra between 1631 and 1648 by order of the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favourite wife, the Taj Mahal is the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

India gate placed in delhi

India Gate, official name Delhi Memorial, originally called All-India War Memorial, dedicated to the troops of British India who died in wars fought between 1914 and 1919.The iconic India Gate, an arch gate made using sandstone,situated in the Rajpath area, Delhi.

It is the city of lord shiva

Shri Kashi Vishwanath temple in Varanasi holds immense significance to the Hindus and is one of the most famous temples in India dedicated to Lord Shiva. The divine shrine stands tall on the Western bank of the holy river Ganga and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas.

Delhi's Red Fort

The Red Fort, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a majestic Mughal-era fort in Delhi, India. Known for its red sandstone walls and intricate architecture, it served as the main residence of Mughal emperors for nearly 200 years.

Namaste, Nice to Meet You

Namaste is a Sanskrit greeting that is commonly used in Indian culture. It literally means "I bow to you" and is a gesture of respect and reverence. It's often accompanied by a slight bow of the head or hands pressed together in front of the chest.

The Bindu's Untold Story

In many Hindu communities, the bindu is a traditional symbol of marriage and marital status. It is often applied with kumkum or sindoor, which are believed to bring good fortune and protect the husband.

The Cow's Divine Presence

In Hindu mythology, the cow is depicted as accompanying several gods and is considered a symbol of wealth, strength, abundance, and divine and natural beneficence. In ancient Hindu texts, the cow is called "Kamdhenu", which means "divine cow" and fulfills all desires. In some scriptures, the cow is identified as the "mother" of all civilization.

Fasting as an Act of Love

Fasting as an act of love is a practice found in many cultures and religions, often rooted in the belief that self-sacrifice and deprivation can strengthen spiritual connections and express deep affection. In some traditions, fasting is seen as a way to purify the body and mind, making one more receptive to divine love.

Colours Of Culture

From splendid paintings to beautiful wood crafts and textiles, India resounds with a rich and unique culture.

The Indian culinary repertoire reflects the cultural diversity of the country. The term “Indian food” denotes a melange of flavours from different parts of the country and showcases centuries of cultural exchange with the far corners of the world

The most important classical traditions of music in India are the Hindustani classical tradition which finds home in the northern and western parts of the country and the Carnatic classical tradition, which is practiced primarily in the southern parts of the country.

Considered to be the oldest dance and an inspiration to all other styles, Bharat Natyam, a temple dance of Tamil Nadu, is an enchanting performance that relates scenes from religious texts and myths. In a series of quick and complicated neat motions.

Festivals in India are a vibrant representation of the culture and ancient traditions of the country. The most prominent celebration is that of Diwali, which is popularly called the festival of lights. Like most festivals in the country, its story is rooted in myths, legends and religion.
